Keyboard reviews -

Cannon Keys - Bakeneko 60

Having not used a 60% board for a few years, it took a bit of adjusting to but I have come to enjoy the size a lot more than I initially expected. The Bakeneko60 is a gummy o-ring mount style keyboard which makes for a nice isolated sound. My only complaint would be that the board can sound a bit hollow without any mods, but this is easily fixed with some sound dampening material. My only note on trying sound dampening with this board is the material has to be pretty thin or else the bounce provided by the o-ring is lost. For my setup, I did the classic tape mod as well as diy two part silicone. This was my first attempt at custom silicone but I think it turned out pretty good, just had to be careful about how much I put in the case so as to not completely remove the benefits of the gummy o-ring isolation.

    Pros -

  • soft typing feel
  • isolated acoustics
  • included stabilizers
  • price

    • cons -

  • hollow sound when stock
  • switches pop out easily at the corners
  • need to fix backspace in via

I would describe the typing experience of this board to be similar to a top mount in terms of stiffness but with the benefits of being isolated. After using keyboards with flex cuts, half plates, or gaskets, this is not as gummy as the mount style naming would make you think. A fun quirk about the flex with this board is when you press on one corner the opposite comes up out of the case slightly. The simplicity of the design is something worth appreciating and makes assembling quite easy especially with hotswap sockets. The only issue with hotswap on a o-ring keyboard is that since the switches are what keeps the whole assembly together (that plus the o-ring pushing up against the corner switches) it is not uncommon for switches to pop out when trying to put the board together meaning the process has to be redone.

As an entry level board I think this is a pretty good deal, you get a lot for the money. I think the bakeneko is also a great chance to try a gummy o-ring board without a huge investment. I would recommend this board to someone who is looking for a budget friendly 60% keyboard as an entry point into the hobby. However if you already have experience with other enthusiast boards I would skip this and look for something that could be considered an "upgrade" unless you really need to try a gummy o-ring keyboard.
